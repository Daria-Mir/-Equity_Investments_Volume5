Module 2

Security Market Indexes

Audio: How Security Market Indexes Really Work

Indexes are the pulse of financial markets. Explore index construction methodologies, including price-weighting, equal-weighting, market-capitalization-weighting, and fundamental-weighting. Contrast how stock splits affect divisors and index values.

Index Weighting Methods
Method Determined By Key Risk / Bias
Price Stock Price High-priced stocks dominate
Equal 1 / N shares Frequent rebalancing costs
Market-Cap Total Value Overweights overvalued stocks
Fundamental Sales / BV / NI Value tilt bias
Module 3

Market Efficiency

Audio: Can You Actually Outsmart the Market?

Examine the Efficient Market Hypothesis (EMH). Distinguish between weak, semi-strong, and strong forms of market efficiency, and understand what they mean for technical analysis, fundamental analysis, and active management. Learn about market anomalies like calendar effects and pricing discrepancies.

Forms of Market Efficiency
W

Weak Form Efficiency

Reflects all historical trading data. Technical Analysis fails.

SS

Semi-Strong Form Efficiency

Reflects all public data (earnings, news). Fundamental Analysis fails.

S

Strong Form Efficiency

Reflects all public & private info. Insider trading fails.

Module 4

Overview of Equity Securities

Audio: The Hidden Machinery of Global Equities

Delve into common stock, preference shares, public versus private equity, and foreign security ownership structures (like ADRs and GDRs). Analyze risk and return characteristics, voting systems (cumulative vs. statutory), and calculate book vs. market values.

Common vs. Preferred Shares
Feature Common Shares Preferred Shares
Voting Rights Yes Generally No
Dividends Variable, Discretionary Fixed Rate, Priority
Liquidation Claim Residual claim (last) Priority over common
Growth Potential High Low (debt-like)

Module 6

Industry & Competitive Analysis

Audio: Why Industry Structure Beats Great Execution

Analyze industry boundaries, competitive forces (Porter's Five Forces), and industry life cycles. Relate macroeconomic external factors to company growth, and learn how to identify generic corporate strategies: cost leadership, differentiation, and focus.

Porter's Generic Strategies

Cost Leadership

Broad scope. Lower costs than competitors (e.g., Walmart).

Differentiation

Broad scope. Unique products that justify premium prices (e.g., Apple).

Cost Focus

Narrow scope. Target specific niche with low cost strategy.

Differentiation Focus

Narrow scope. Target niche with highly customized product.

Module 7

Company Analysis - Forecasting

Audio: How Professionals Forecast Company Financial Results

Explore modeling techniques used by analysts. Contrast top-down and bottom-up forecasting models for revenues. Model operating costs, working capital accounts, capital expenditures, and run scenario analysis to stress test financial assumptions.

Forecasting Revenue Approaches
Approach Starting Point Best Suited For
Top-Down GDP / Industry Growth Mature, highly-correlated markets
Bottom-Up Unit Volumes & Prices Retailers, single-product niches
Hybrid Both Combined Diversified global corporations
Module 8

Equity Valuation

Audio: Three Models for Calculating Intrinsic Value

Discover the ultimate goal of equity research: determining intrinsic value. Learn how to apply Present Value models (Dividend Discount and Free Cash Flow models), Multiplier models (P/E, P/B, EV/EBITDA), and Asset-based valuations.

Valuation Model Classifications
Model Class Core Input / Focus Key Metric
Present Value Discounted cash flows / div Cost of Equity (r), Growth (g)
Multipliers Comparable multiples / size P/E, EV/EBITDA, P/B
Asset-Based Fair value of net assets Assets minus Liabilities
